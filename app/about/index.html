About

I recently left my job at Lick Creative to jump into learning to code full-time. I've been learning web dev on my own for the last 18 months or so, including building the Lick website using Jekyll. I like performance, accessibility, and pushing the limits of what can be done with CSS

Experience

Lick Creative

2013-2016

I joined Lick as part of a new initiative to drive innovation and technology within the business. The agency had a reputation for specialising in static printed point-of-sale, which they wanted to change. My original role was to educate the creative teams on new technologies that could offer something more to our clients. I was also responsible for maintaining the website and company blog, as well as our social media accounts.

I then moved into a more hands-on position, helping to integrate technology into our projects, communicating directly with clients and the creative teams to bring some innovation into our work. We successfully ran everything from one-off experiential events to technology-driven marketing campaigns and are beginning to provide customer analytics and tracking for our retail clients.

Education

University of Manchester

2009-2012

Studying for a BA in Philosophy, Politics & Economics

I spent three years reading PPE in Manchester. When I left school I had a variety of interests and wanted to pursue something completely new, so I chose three subjects instead of one. I narrowed this down over the course of the degree and ended up focusing on political philosophy and ethics.

I eventually wrote my dissertation on whether we should always save the largest group of people, receiving a First class grade for the paper. My degree required a good deal of organisational skill in reconciling the many varied disciplines and keeping track of so many differing projects, which is something that has definitely come in handy so far in my many roles at Lick.
